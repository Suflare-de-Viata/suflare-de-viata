Guidings for the authors

As I mentioned on the first page of the site, for publishing in the section of Studies we ensure peer review. For the other sections (Recensions, Essays, and Preaches), there are received articles of Orthodox thought. There are not received the materials that are insuficiently elaborated on scientific method (for the Studies) or on logic (for the other sections). Neither are they received texts with insults against any person or social, ethnical, or religious categories. There may be some situations when we can't receive a material for it does not fit into the preoccupations of our magazine or we decline our competence to evaluate it. To avoid sending a material either not fit or beyond our competences, the author should notice, at first, that our large domains of interest are: (1) history, (2) linguistics and translations (see below in which terms ), and (3) Orthodox theology. The historical studies include those on Romanian and universal history, history of the religions, and Church history (Romanian and universal). If someone would send a material well fit into one of the mentioned fields, but it still surpasses our area of expertize for peer review or our ability for editorial management, this situation will be communicated to the author.

The ARTICLES may be sent in: Romanian, English, French, Spanish, or Italian.

The ARTICLES will be sent in a Word document (.docx, not otherwise ): font Times New Roman, 12, 1.15 (Line spacing ), justify. It may be used bold or italic text, but not excessively. The first paragraph of the manuscript sent for publishing will begin with the full name of the author, followed by colon, space, and the title of the article. If this title (and the article) is any other language, it will be followed by slash and a translation of the title in English. The first paragraph of the article and the titles of its sections (normally indented) appear bold. Additionaly, if necessary, they may be used maximum two orders of subtitles: bold italic, and italic. In the section with Studies, the footnotes must be used for bibliography. The first footnote, attached to the end title of the "Abstract" section, mentions the author's email and, optionally, his/her academic affiliation and/or other information about the author. The articles are without final bibliography. There are not allowed other formattings (as bullets, numberings - except those atomatically given for the footnotes ), and active Reviews either.

CITATION IN FOOTNOTES:

(1) Author, Title, Publishing House, Publishing Place, Publishing Year, Page.

E. g.: (Pr. Prof. Dr.) Dumitru Stăniloae, Teologia Dogmatică Ortodoxă, Ed. IBMBOR, Bucharest, Vol. 1, 1996, p. 25.

(2) Author, "Article's Title", Magazine's Title (complete), Number, Year, Page.

E. g.: Pr. Prof. Dr. Mircea Basarab, "Autoritatea Sfintei Scripturi în Biserica Ortodoxă", in Ortodoxia, XXXII, no. 2, 1980, (pp. 221-224), p. 222. [Pages: if there is a quotation or a punctual citation, there must appear both the limit pages of the article and the page(s) of the exact reference.]

As we may notice in the above examples, the last names of teh authors will appear in Small Caps (Ctrl+Shift+k in Word). The last name of the author of the manuscript will appear in the same way, in the first paragraph of the material.

For the online articles, their link should be apparent, followed by the date of accessing, between parantheses.

It may be used Ibid. when repeating the previous work and Id. when repeating the previous author with a different work. It will not be used op. cit., but at the second citing should appear only the last name of the author and a shortened title, followed by ellipsis.

Displaying the ecclsiastical or scientifical title of an author is optional, but it is recommended that a priest, monk or saint to be mentioned at least briefly as such, like Fr., St., or S., in Latin, as in S. Gregorius Nyssenus, as it appears in Migne or other sources.

It is also accepted the full style in the Cambridge Manual of Style, but only for the articles completely written in English (for which it is best fit).

Any article will have an Abstract in English (optional in other of the mentioned languages), Keywords in English, but also in the language in which the article was written. The Abstract's title is bold, as any other section title. Its content is with font size 10, line spacing 1.15. It has maximum 500 words. Any quotation longer than 2 lines must be separated from the rest of the text, in a new paragraph, between quotation marks, with font size 10, line spacing also 1.15. Images, including maps, also tables and diagrams can be either inserted in the Word document, or sent separatelly. In the last case, in the text, in a distinctive paragraph, with a blank paragraph above and a blank one below, it is mentioned: <Table 1>, <Map 1>, etc., for the place where each element must be inserted.

TRANSLATIONS. Translations may be done, in order to fall under the grasp of our fellow peer reviewers, from and into any of the languages mentioned above: Romanian, English, French, or Italian. Exceptionally, German may be added. There are also welcomed translations from classical languages: Greek and Latin. Biblical Hebrew could be added to this endeavor. Especially translations from the last three languages will be scrupulously verified by reviewers. However, again, any such essential work will be welcomed. The author might proceed in three ways, regarding the translations. (1) Give the precise references for the translated text and submit it for publication. (2) Alternate source text with translated text, in different sections, with clear connection between them, so the reader will know which is translated where. (3) Place source text and translation(s) (e. g., from a classical language in one or two modern languages) in parallel columns of one or more tables. To avoid their displacing, sectioning them at less then an A4 page is recommended. There will not be used pages formatted on columns, but only tables! The tables used to display parallel texts will have all their borders invisible. (Table Design - Borders - No Border.) It is also recommended to reduce cells margins at 0.05.
